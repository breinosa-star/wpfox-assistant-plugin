─── COLOR DESIGN ───

Six required colors and their roles in the theme:

primary    → Navigation bar background, hero gradient start, button backgrounds, link color.
             MUST be dark enough for white (#ffffff) text — WCAG AA minimum contrast.
secondary  → Hero gradient end, section tint background (92% white blend), alternate accents.
accent     → CTA band gradient, button hover state, footer top-border highlight.
background → Page canvas and card backgrounds (typically #ffffff or near-white).
text       → Body copy and headings on light backgrounds. Must achieve 4.5:1 contrast on background.
muted      → Captions, metadata, secondary paragraphs, borders.

LOGO COLORS TAKE PRIORITY:
- When logo colors are provided, they are the brand — do not substitute unrelated colors.
- If logo primary is dark enough for white text: use it as theme primary.
- If logo primary is too light for white text: use it as accent, derive a darker shade for primary.
- Logo accent MUST appear in theme accent.
- Industry color guidance applies ONLY when no logo colors are provided.

─── STYLE ARCHETYPE ───

Choose exactly one style_archetype that matches the brand's visual character.
The archetype overrides Bootstrap's design tokens (border-radius, shadows, card style, button shape)
to give the entire theme a consistent visual personality.

  soft-modern    Gentle shadows, 8-12px radius, clean and professional.
                 Best: SaaS, fintech, B2B software, productivity tools.

  flat-minimal   No shadows, sharp corners (2px), border-defined layout.
                 Best: Developer tools, law/consulting, design-forward agencies, Notion-like tools.

  glassmorphism  Frosted glass cards, backdrop-filter blur, pill buttons, vivid gradients.
                 Best: AI products, creative SaaS, tech startups, apps.

  editorial      Square corners, zero shadows, top-rule cards, caps buttons, type-forward.
                 Best: Media, publishing, architecture studios, luxury brands, law firms.

  dark-luxury    Dark canvas (#0c0c0a), warm accent on dark, refined spacing, premium feel.
                 Best: Luxury brands, premium hospitality, high-end real estate, private equity.

  brutalist      Heavy 2px borders, hard offset shadows (no blur), all-caps, chunky weight.
                 Best: Creative agencies, music labels, streetwear, avant-garde startups.

  organic        Very rounded (20-24px), pill buttons, soft warm shadows, no borders.
                 Best: Wellness, health, food, community platforms, family services.

  technical      Small radius (4px), monospace accents, border-defined, functional over decorative.
                 Best: Developer tools, APIs, infrastructure products, data dashboards.

Archetype × brand color examples:
- soft-modern + navy primary   → clean SaaS (like Stripe)
- glassmorphism + deep purple  → modern AI/tech product
- editorial + forest green     → luxury lifestyle brand
- dark-luxury + champagne      → premium hospitality
- brutalist + red/black        → avant-garde creative agency
- organic + teal               → wellness or health platform
- technical + dark indigo      → developer infrastructure tool
- flat-minimal + charcoal      → consulting or law firm

─── TYPOGRAPHY ───

Heading + body font pairings by style:
- Editorial / Luxury: Playfair Display + Inter
- Bold / Modern: Montserrat + Lato
- Clean / Startup: Outfit + DM Sans
- Technical / Developer: Space Grotesk + Inter
- Warm / Approachable: Merriweather + Source Sans Pro
- High-end / Architecture: Cormorant Garamond + Raleway
- Minimalist / Corporate: Inter + Inter

heading_letter_spacing rules:
- Geometric sans (Montserrat, Outfit, Inter, Space Grotesk, DM Sans): "-0.03em" to "-0.02em"
- Serif headings (Playfair Display, Merriweather, Cormorant Garamond): "0.01em" to "0.02em"
- Any uppercase headings: always "0.06em" to "0.12em" regardless of font category

heading_text_transform: Use "none" for most styles. Use "uppercase" only for brutalist, editorial,
or technical archetypes with short headings (1–4 words).

─── CONVERSION FUNNEL SEQUENCING ───

The order of slugs in templates["front-page.html"].patterns determines the homepage funnel. Always follow this sequence:

  1. ATTENTION   — Always first. Exactly one hero layout. Never skip.
  2. CREDIBILITY — Near the top. Builds trust before explaining value.
                   Use: logo-strip, stats-row, review-stars-row, press-mentions.
  3. VALUE PROP  — Explain what you do and why it is better.
                   Use: three-column-cards, six-icon-grid, image-checklist-split, numbered-steps, image-text-split.
  4. PROOF       — Reinforce with social evidence. Never skip.
                   Use: testimonials-grid, testimonials-single, case-study-grid.
  5. DETAIL      — Pricing, process, FAQ, or differentiation.
                   Use: three-tier-pricing, two-tier-pricing, comparison-table, accordion-faq.
  6. CONVERSION  — Always last. Exactly one CTA. Never skip.
                   Use: gradient-cta-band, light-dual-cta, newsletter-form.

Rules:
- Minimum 4 patterns, maximum 8 patterns in front-page.html.
- Never two heroes, never two consecutive testimonial patterns, never two CTAs.
- Pricing always after value proposition — never before features.
- Skip stages that don't apply (e.g. a portfolio site may skip pricing).
